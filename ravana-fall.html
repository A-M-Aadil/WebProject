Ravana Ella Waterfall

Description:

Ravana Falls is one of the most popular waterfalls in Sri Lanka. It is located in the hill country town of Ella, and is easily accessible by road or train. The waterfall is named after Ravana, the demon king of Lanka in the Hindu epic Ramayana. Ravana Falls is one of the widest waterfalls in Sri Lanka. The waterfall is surrounded by lush greenery and is a popular spot for swimming and photography.

Height: 25 m | (82 ft)

Things to do:
🔸 Take a swim in the pool at the base of the waterfall.
🔸 Hike to the top of the waterfall for stunning views of the surrounding      countryside.
🔸 Visit the Ravana Ella Cave.
🔸 Relax and enjoy the scenery.

Location: Ella, Badulla District

Directions:
🔸Ravana Falls is located about 6 km (3.7 mi) from Ella Railway Station. To get to the waterfall, you can take a tuk-tuk or walk from the     town. If you are coming from Badulla, you can take the Badulla-Bandarawela road and turn left at the Kubalwela junction.

Additional information:
🔸 According to legend, Ravana used to hide behind the waterfall after kidnapping Sita, the wife of Rama.
🔸 The caves behind the waterfall are now known as the Ravana Ella Cave.
🔸 The best time to visit Ravana Falls is during the rainy season, from May to October.
🔸 The entrance fee to the waterfall is Rs. 50 per person.
🔸 There are no changing rooms or showers at the waterfall.
🔸 There are a few small shops and restaurants near the waterfall.
